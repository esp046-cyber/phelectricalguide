‚ö° System Types

1 Conventional Fire Alarm System
  • Technology: Zone-based detection
  • How it works: Devices grouped in zones, panel shows which zone triggered
  • Pros: Lower cost, simpler installation
  • Cons: No individual device identification
  • Best for: Small to medium buildings
  • Cost: ‚Ç±30,000-80,000 for small building

  • Technology: Each device has unique address

  • Technology: Radio frequency communication
  • How it works: Devices communicate wirelessly with panel
  • Pros: Minimal wiring, ideal for heritage/difficult buildings
  • Cons: Battery maintenance, radio interference risk
  • Best for: Retrofits, temporary installations

‚ö° Power Quality Issues

Power quality problems can damage equipment, reduce efficiency, and cause safety hazards. Understanding and fixing these issues is crucial for proper electrical maintenance.

üìâ Common Power Quality Problems

Symptoms: Lights dim, motors slow down, appliances malfunction

  • Balance loads across phases
  • Check and tighten all connections
  • Install voltage regulator/stabilizer

Symptoms: Equipment damage, blown fuses, shortened appliance life

Solutions:

  • Install whole-house surge protector
  • Use point-of-use surge protectors
  • Proper lightning protection system
  • Regular grounding system maintenance
3 Harmonics

Symptoms: Overheating transformers, neutral overload, equipment malfunction

Causes: Non-linear loads (computers, LED lights, inverters)

Solutions:

  • Install harmonic filters
  • Use K-rated transformers
  • Upsize neutral conductor
  • Isolate sensitive equipment
4 Low Power Factor

Symptoms: High electricity bills, overheating equipment, voltage drop

Causes: Inductive loads (motors, transformers), fluorescent lights

Solutions:

  • Install power factor correction capacitors
  • Replace old motors with high-efficiency models
  • Use electronic ballasts for lighting
  • Balance loads properly
